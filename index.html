Computer science

  1. Algorithms and Data Structures: The foundation of computer science, focusing on efficient problem-solving techniques and organizing data.
  2. Programming Languages: The study of languages used to communicate with computers, like Python, Java, C++, and JavaScript.
  3. Operating Systems: The study of systems that manage computer hardware and software resources, like Windows, macOS, and Linux.
  4. Databases: The design and management of systems that store and retrieve data, including SQL, NoSQL, and data warehousing.
  5. Software Engineering: The principles and practices of designing, developing, testing, and maintaining software systems.
  6. Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML): The development of systems that can learn from data, recognize patterns, and make decisions.
  7. Networks and Security: The study of communication systems and the methods used to secure data and systems from attacks.
  8. Web Development: The creation of websites and web applications, involving front-end (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) and back-end (server, database) development.
  9. Human-Computer Interaction (HCI): The study of how people interact with computers and how to design interfaces that are user-friendly.
  10. Theoretical Computer Science: The study of computational theory, including topics like automata, formal languages, and complexity theory.
  11. Cybersecurity: The protection of computer systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access or attacks.

Artificial intelligence

Artificial intelligence (AI) is a set of technologies that enable computers to perform a variety of advanced functions, including the ability to see, understand and translate spoken and written language, analyze data, make recommendations, and more. AI is the backbone of innovation in modern computing, unlocking value for individuals and businesses. For example, optical character recognition (OCR) uses AI to extract text and data from images and documents, turns unstructured content into business-ready structured data, and unlocks valuable insights.
